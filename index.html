I was born in Boulder, Colorado but my art work is profoundly inspired and impacted by Latin influences, thanks to experiences gathered studying in Oaxaca, Mexico, as well as an aggregate of 13 years living in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

The 1960's Tropicália art movement, while widely recognized for the musical evolutions that took place, is one that my art not reflects and which I use as a source of inspiration. Through this movement's lens I like to use my paintings as a vehicle to reminisce the central Brazilian cultural focal points, as well as the political undercurrents.

The bright color schemes and whimsical tones also have striking similarities and draw clear connections to this revolutionary art movement that changed Brazilian artistic expressions and the social atmosphere of the time.

Colors have always seemed to evoke certain emotions and feelings, so they are wielded unsparingly to give breath to these same reactions in the viewers.

»May this art reach you and move you, just as it moves the artist to create it.

"When asked about my art,
the most important elements are the colors and the emotions."
